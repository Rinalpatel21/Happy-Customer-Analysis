# Happy Customer Analysis

## Project Overview
In the competitive logistics and delivery sector, understanding the "why" behind customer satisfaction is critical. This project, completed during the Apziva AI Residency, aims to help a logistics startup (ACME) predict customer happiness based on survey responses.



## Objective
- Analyze customer survey data to uncover patterns driving satisfaction  
- Build predictive models to classify customers as Happy (1) or Unhappy (0)  
- Identify the most influential features impacting customer happiness  



## Dataset Description
The analysis uses the ACME-HappinessSurvey2020.csv dataset, consisting of 126 observations. Customers rated their experience on a scale of 1–5 across six key metrics:

- X1: Order delivered on time  
- X2: Contents of order were as expected  
- X3: I ordered everything I wanted to order  
- X4: I paid a good price for my order  
- X5: I am satisfied with my courier  
- X6: The app makes ordering easy for me  
- Y (Target): Customer Happiness (0 = Unhappy, 1 = Happy)  



## Project Workflow
The project workflow can be summarized in the following steps:

1. Data Collection: The team gathered customer satisfaction data from various sources, including customer surveys, social media, and customer support tickets.  

2. Data Preprocessing: The raw data was cleaned, normalized, and transformed to prepare it for analysis. This involved handling missing values, removing outliers, and converting data into a suitable format for further processing.  

3. Feature Engineering: Relevant features were extracted from the data, such as customer demographics, purchase history, and sentiment analysis of customer feedback.  

4. Model Development: Several machine learning models, including regression and classification algorithms, were trained and tested to identify the key drivers of customer satisfaction.  

5. Model Evaluation: The performance of the models was evaluated using appropriate metrics, such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score, to ensure the reliability and robustness of the results.  

6. Insights and Recommendations: The insights gained from the analysis were summarized, and actionable recommendations were provided to help the client improve their customer experience and increase customer retention.

## Results
- Successfully built a classification model to predict customer happiness  
- Derived actionable insights that businesses can use to improve customer experience  
- Demonstrated the importance of operational efficiency and pricing strategy  



## Insights and Recommendations
- Enhance Courier Experience: Final touchpoint has the highest impact—train couriers, track ratings, and reward top performers to quickly boost customer satisfaction.  

- Improve Delivery Reliability: Optimize routes, enable real-time tracking, and communicate delays proactively to build trust and reduce frustration.  

- Reduce Expectation Gaps: Align orders with expectations through better product info, real-time inventory, and early communication on substitutions.  

- Remove X6 Feature: “Ease of ordering” adds minimal predictive value (~8%) and can be removed without impacting model performance. Business Note: Low model impact ≠ low importance—app usability matters, but it doesn’t differentiate happy vs. unhappy customers in this dataset.
